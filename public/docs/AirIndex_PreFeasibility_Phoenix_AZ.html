EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Phoenix scores 55/100 on the AirIndex Market Readiness Index — MODERATE tier, ranking #5 nationally. The market has strong foundational elements: Joby Aviation is actively conducting autonomous flight testing, FAA LAANC coverage is confirmed, and Arizona has the most advanced multi-bill AAM legislative package in the country right now. The single biggest constraint is infrastructure: no approved vertiports and no municipal zoning framework for vertiport siting. SB1827 is the highest-leverage item in the index — passage moves Phoenix from MODERATE to the threshold of ADVANCED and fundamentally changes the infrastructure investment conversation.

THE ARIZONA LEGISLATIVE PACKAGE

Arizona's coordinated three-bill approach mirrors the legislative strategies that preceded rapid score acceleration in Texas and Florida. This is not a single exploratory bill — it's a comprehensive AAM governance framework.

SB1827 Office of Advanced Air Mobility
TRANSMIT TO HOUSE

Creates a dedicated state office for AAM coordination — the institutional framework that operators and developers need as a counterparty. Establishes Arizona as a proactive AAM state, similar to Texas's TXDOT Aviation Division role under HB 1735.

Impact: +5 pts (LEG factor) · Enables posture upgrade
SB1819 Vertiports; design; zoning
CONSENT CALENDAR

Creates the statewide zoning framework for vertiport siting and design standards. This is the prerequisite for vertiport development — without it, every project requires ad-hoc municipal approval. Consent calendar status suggests bipartisan support.

Impact: Enables +15 pts (ZONE factor) upon municipal adoption · Prerequisite for vertiport approval (+20 pts)
SB1826 AAM appropriation
HOUSE 2ND READING

Dedicates state funding to AAM development. Signals long-term commitment — this is the "put money behind it" bill. Combined with SB1827's institutional framework, it creates both the office and the budget to execute.

Impact: Strengthens regulatory posture signal · Federal grant leverage

METHODOLOGY

AirIndex scores U.S. metro markets on UAM readiness using seven weighted factors totaling 100 points. All data is traced to primary sources: FAA LAANC (UAS Facility Map), Federal Register (regulatory notices), LegiScan (state legislation tracking), and SEC EDGAR (operator filings). An AI classification layer parses incoming signals for factor relevance, with human review at multiple confidence tiers. Scores update automatically when qualifying events are detected and verified.

The model measures current verified conditions, not predictions. It does not forecast commercialization timelines, assess operator financial viability, or evaluate aviation safety outcomes. Scores reflect the regulatory, legislative, infrastructure, and operator conditions that determine market readiness for commercial eVTOL operations.

Full methodology: airindex.io/methodology · Classification accuracy: ~89% (March 2026 audit)
