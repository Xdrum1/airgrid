EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Charlotte scores 35/100 on the AirIndex Market Readiness Index — EARLY tier, ranking #10 nationally. The market has genuine demand-side strengths that most EARLY-tier markets lack: CLT is the 6th busiest airport in the US, the banking and finance concentration creates natural executive shuttle demand, and Charlotte is the fastest-growing major city in the Southeast. NC DOT's participation in the FAA AAM Integration Pilot Program is a meaningful federal signal. The constraint is structural: no state legislation, no operator commitment, and no vertiport infrastructure. The demand case is strong — the supply-side framework hasn't caught up.

THE NORTH CAROLINA LEGISLATION GAP

North Carolina is one of the few states with active federal AAM engagement (through NC DOT's pilot program) but no state-level enabling legislation. This creates an asymmetry: the federal signal is strong, but operators and infrastructure developers cannot act without the state legal framework.

NORTH CAROLINA: NO LEGISLATION

NC DOT is at the federal table. Charlotte has the demand profile. CLT airport's long-range plan references UAM. The Charlotte Future Mobility Task Force exists. But without a state enabling framework, operators have no legal certainty, developers have no zoning pathway, and the federal engagement cannot convert into infrastructure investment. One bill changes Charlotte's trajectory.

METHODOLOGY

AirIndex scores U.S. metro markets on UAM readiness using seven weighted factors totaling 100 points. All data is traced to primary sources: FAA LAANC (UAS Facility Map), Federal Register (regulatory notices), LegiScan (state legislation tracking), and SEC EDGAR (operator filings). An AI classification layer parses incoming signals for factor relevance, with human review at multiple confidence tiers. Scores update automatically when qualifying events are detected and verified.

The model measures current verified conditions, not predictions. It does not forecast commercialization timelines, assess operator financial viability, or evaluate aviation safety outcomes. Scores reflect the regulatory, legislative, infrastructure, and operator conditions that determine market readiness for commercial eVTOL operations.
