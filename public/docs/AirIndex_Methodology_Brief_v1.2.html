1 Purpose and Scope

AirIndex scores U.S. cities on their readiness for commercial eVTOL (electric vertical takeoff and landing) operations using a systematic, data-driven methodology — not editorial judgment. The index is designed to give operators, city planners, investors, airport authorities, and policy researchers a consistent, auditable framework for comparing market readiness across geographies.

The index currently covers 21 U.S. markets, scored on a 0–100 scale across seven weighted factors. Scores are updated automatically when qualifying regulatory, legislative, or operator events are detected by the ingestion pipeline, with human review triggered for medium and low-confidence classifications.

What the index does not measure: AirIndex does not predict commercialization timelines, assess operator financial viability, or evaluate aviation safety outcomes. Scores reflect market readiness conditions — not guarantees of eVTOL deployment.

2 Scoring Framework

Each city is scored across seven factors. Six factors are binary (present/absent for full points). One factor — State Legislation — uses a graduated three-tier model introduced in v1.2.1. The maximum possible score is 100.

SCORING FACTORPOINTSRATIONALE
Active Pilot Program20 ptsPresence of a sanctioned UAM/eVTOL pilot program — the strongest single indicator of near-term commercial readiness.
Approved Vertiport20 ptsAt least one vertiport has received regulatory approval, indicating physical infrastructure commitment.
Active Operator Presence15 ptsA certified eVTOL operator has announced or commenced operations in the market.
Vertiport Zoning15 ptsMunicipal or county zoning frameworks explicitly accommodate vertiport infrastructure.
Regulatory Posture10 ptsThe local regulatory environment is demonstrably favorable to UAM operations based on FAA and municipal signals.
State Legislation10 ptsGraduated three-tier model: enacted (10 pts) — state has passed UAM/eVTOL legislation; actively_moving (5 pts) — bills in late legislative stages (committee passage, floor votes, 2nd reading); none (0 pts). Source: LegiScan primary legislative data.
LAANC Coverage10 ptsLow Altitude Authorization and Notification Capability (LAANC) is active in the market area, enabling automated airspace access.

3 Data Sources

AirIndex ingests data from four primary sources. Each source feeds a distinct category of scoring signal. As of March 2026, the active dataset contains 1,403 ingested records across all four sources.

Federal Register6.0% · 84 records

FAA rulings, NOPRMs, Part 135 applications, Special Federal Aviation Regulations (SFARs), and airspace notices. The most authoritative government source for regulatory posture and pilot program signals. Active lookback window: 730 days (2 years). 12 search terms including "eVTOL Integration Pilot Program" and FAA docket monitoring. Updated: daily cron ingestion.

Federal Register coverage expanded from 5 to 84 records in March 2026 via pagination fix, double-filter removal, and search term expansion (v1.1). Additional search terms for FAA eVTOL/AAM Integration Pilot Program added March 14, 2026 (v1.2).
LegiScan28.4% · 399 records

State-level UAM and UAS legislation across all 50 states — bills, resolutions, and committee actions. Primary source for the State Legislation scoring factor, including graduated status assessment (enacted vs. actively_moving vs. none). Coverage filters applied to UAM-relevant legislative activity.

SEC EDGAR5.6% · 79 records

Operator filings from publicly traded eVTOL companies including Joby Aviation and Archer Aviation (8-Ks, 10-Qs, and material event disclosures). Used to detect operator market expansion, partnership announcements, and certification milestones.

Operator News59.9% · 841 records

Curated operator announcements, market expansions, and industry milestones from primary sources including company press releases and aviation trade publications. Selection criteria: announcements must reference a specific market, regulatory action, or operational milestone. General financial coverage and stock analysis are excluded.

Operator News is a watch list and intelligence signal source. Per the Score Promotion Policy (Section 6), news coverage alone does not qualify for automatic score promotion on primary-source-required factors.

4 Classification Engine

Raw ingested records are processed by an AI classification engine (Claude Haiku 4.5) that assigns each record an event type, confidence level, affected city tags, and relevant scoring factors. This classification drives scoring override recommendations — the mechanism by which new information updates city scores.

Confidence Tiering

Every classification result includes a confidence field with one of three values. The confidence level determines how the record moves through the pipeline:

CONFIDENCEVOLUMEBEHAVIORHUMAN REVIEW
HIGH84%Classification flows to auto-reviewer. High-confidence overrides evaluated for automatic score promotion (subject to source quality requirements).Only if auto-reviewer rejects
MEDIUM12%Flagged for human review queue. Not promoted to scoring automatically.Required before any score change
NEEDS REVIEW4%Manual intervention required. Record held pending human classification decision.Always required

Audit Trail

Every classification and scoring decision is fully logged. The system maintains three persistent audit tables:

  • ClassificationResult — Stores every classification with raw response, confidence level, model used, prompt version, and factors JSON.
  • ScoringOverride — Tracks every override with confidence, reason, source record ID, applied timestamp, and superseded timestamp.
  • AutoReviewResult — Logs every auto-review decision with reasoning, confidence, application status, and source content.
Every score change is traceable to a specific source record, classification result, and override decision — including the timestamp, confidence level, and reasoning. This audit trail is maintained indefinitely and available for methodology review upon request.

6 Score Promotion Policy

Established March 2026. Score changes require source documentation that meets a two-tier quality hierarchy. This policy is enforced automatically in the classification pipeline and auto-reviewer.

Two-Source Hierarchy

SOURCE TIEREXAMPLESELIGIBLE ACTIONS
Primary / OfficialFederal Register filings, FAA documentation, DOT notices, LegiScan legislative records, state legislature records, SEC EDGAR filings, official .gov sourcesAutomatic score promotion at high confidence. Full scoring factor changes including hasActivePilotProgram, hasVertiportZoning, stateLegislationStatus.
Secondary / NewsCredible local journalism (WRAL, NY Post), aviation trade publications, operator press releases, industry aggregatorsWatch list trigger and elevated activity signal only. Does not qualify for automatic score promotion on primary-source-required factors. Suitable for activeOperators and regulatoryPosture changes.

Factors Requiring Primary Source Documentation

FACTORWEIGHTREQUIRED SOURCERATIONALE
hasActivePilotProgram20 ptsFederal Register filing or direct FAA documentation20-point weight with significant tier implications warrants primary source confirmation
hasVertiportZoning15 ptsOfficial city/county zoning records or council minutesZoning changes require municipal documentation
stateLegislationStatus10 ptsLegiScan or official state legislature recordsLegislative status must be traceable to bill records
Policy rationale: Multi-source news coverage of FAA programs constitutes strong signal and appropriately triggers watch list monitoring. However, pilot program factor changes carry 20-point weight and can produce tier-changing score movements (e.g., Charlotte 15→35, New York 70→90). Score changes of this magnitude require primary source documentation on the audit trail. News coverage routes to watch list; Federal Register filings route to scoring.

7 Market Watch List

Launched March 14, 2026. The Market Watch List is an automated, pipeline-driven intelligence layer that identifies markets showing signals that haven't fully moved their scores yet. Watch list status is not a score change — it is early intelligence.

Trigger Types

TRIGGERCRITERIAEXAMPLE
LEGISLATIONstateLegislationStatus === "actively_moving"Phoenix (AZ SB1827/SB1819/SB1826), Columbus (OH HR304/HB251), Chicago (IL HB3190)
PIPELINE_OVERRIDEUnresolved high/medium confidence scoring overrides pending reviewLos Angeles, Miami, Houston — pending operator or regulatory overrides
ELEVATED_ACTIVITY3+ relevant classifications in 30 days with no score movementCharlotte (NC DOT + FAA AAM pilot program news), New York (FAA air taxi test program news)

Watch List Lifecycle

Entries are evaluated daily as part of the 06:00 UTC cron cycle. Entries auto-resolve when their trigger conditions no longer qualify — either because the underlying signal resolved, the score moved to reflect the change, or legislation was enacted. Resolution reasons are logged for audit purposes.

Current state (March 14, 2026): 15 active entries across 10 markets. Includes 2 markets (Charlotte, New York) with elevated activity from news coverage of FAA eVTOL/AAM Integration Pilot Program participant selections — pending Federal Register confirmation per Score Promotion Policy.

8 Score Updates and Versioning

Scores update when the classification pipeline surfaces a high-confidence override from a primary source that the auto-reviewer promotes to active status. The pipeline runs on a daily cron schedule (06:00 UTC). All score changes are logged with source, timestamp, and reasoning.

9 Limitations and Ongoing Development

Transparency about current limitations is central to AirIndex's methodology philosophy. The following limitations are acknowledged and tracked with resolution status.

Operator News Manual Curation Dependency

The largest data source by volume (59.9%) relies on curated RSS feeds. While selection criteria are documented, the source is more dependent on active curation than fully automated sources. Full automation of this source is a V2 priority.

V2
Classification Feedback Loop

Manual review decisions (approve/reject overrides) are stored in AutoReviewResult but are not yet used to fine-tune classification prompts automatically. This feedback loop will be closed in V2/V3 when sufficient decisions have accumulated.

V2/V3
False Negative Rate on Stock-Framed Headlines

High-confidence classifications occasionally dropped real regulatory signals when articles framed underlying events in financial rather than regulatory terms. Resolved in v1.2 prompt update — 7/7 stock-framed test cases correctly filtered. Remaining edge case: DOT "Future of Flight" pilot program under-classified when wrapped in stock commentary (v1.3 target).

RESOLVED v1.2
Metropolitan Area Mapping

Geographic city tagging previously did not fully account for metropolitan areas (e.g., Oakland → san_francisco). Resolved in v1.2 with 17 metro area geographic mappings added to classification prompt. 6/6 Bay Area test cases correctly mapped.

RESOLVED v1.2
URL Deduplication

Some articles appear through multiple RSS feeds or with variant URLs, causing duplicate classifications. Partial improvement in v1.2 via pre-classification URL dedup. Full domain-level normalization (including marketscreener.com redirect handling) planned for v1.3.

PARTIAL
U.S. Markets Only

The current index covers 21 U.S. markets. International expansion (UAE, United Kingdom, Singapore, and parts of Asia) is under consideration for a future version. The scoring methodology is designed to be adaptable to international markets.

V2
Graduated Scoring Factors

Six of seven scoring factors remain binary (present/absent). State Legislation was converted to a graduated three-tier model in v1.2.1. Expansion of graduated scoring to additional factors (e.g., regulatoryPosture, operator presence intensity) is under consideration for a future methodology version.
