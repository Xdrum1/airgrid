WHAT THIS MEANS FOR DFW

DFW's position at 100 is not threatened — no market is close to matching the full-stack readiness (legislation + zoning + vertiports + operator + pilot program). But the competitive landscape for operator attention is shifting. San Francisco went from 40 to 75 in one quarter. Three Texas markets now score 55+ under the same HB 1735 framework. Phoenix could reach 80 if Arizona's bills pass this session. The markets that were 60+ points behind DFW eighteen months ago are closing to within 25–45 points. For DFW, the strategic question isn't whether it stays #1 — it's whether the operator pipeline that 100-score attracts gets diluted as more markets cross the MODERATE threshold and compete for the same operator commitments.
