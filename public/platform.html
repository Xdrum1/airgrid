The Opportunity

Urban air mobility is no longer a concept — it's a market being built in real time. Joby Aviation launched commercial operations in Dubai in February 2026. US cities are next.

The projected market exceeds $1 trillion. Joby, Archer, Wisk, and Volocopter are racing toward FAA certification. Cities from Los Angeles to Dallas are writing vertiport zoning codes and designating flight corridors. Infrastructure decisions that will shape this industry for decades are being made right now.

But the data driving these decisions is fragmented — scattered across FAA databases, state legislatures, city planning documents, SEC filings, and press releases. No single source aggregates it. No benchmark exists to compare market readiness.

AirIndex changes that.

What is AirIndex

AirIndex is the independent market readiness rating system for Urban Air Mobility. We rate U.S. cities across seven factors, producing a single, continuously updated readiness score built on primary regulatory and financial data.

At its core is the AirIndex Readiness Score: a 0–100 composite index that benchmarks every tracked market across seven weighted factors — from active pilot programs and vertiport permits to regulatory posture and FAA airspace coverage. The score is designed to be cited, compared, and tracked over time.

Beyond scoring, the platform tracks flight corridors, monitors regulatory filings, maps vertiport infrastructure, and delivers change alerts — giving investors, operators, and city planners the same unified view of a market that didn't exist before.

How the Score Works

Each market is evaluated across seven factors, weighted by their signal strength for commercial readiness. Two factors use graduated multi-tier models (State Legislation, Weather Intelligence); the other five are binary. The total produces a 0–100 score mapped to four tiers.
