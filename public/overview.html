What is AirIndex?

AirIndex is the independent market readiness rating system for Urban Air Mobility. We rate U.S. cities across seven factors — regulatory environment, infrastructure development, airspace integration, operator presence, public acceptance, investment activity, and economic conditions — producing a single, continuously updated readiness score for each market.

Our ratings are built on primary data: FAA filings, SEC EDGAR, the Federal Register, state legislative records, and operator activity. Every score change is sourced, timestamped, and traceable. We do not rate cities based on press releases or projections. We rate them on what is actually happening.

The Problem

The UAM industry is approaching a critical inflection point — Joby, Archer, and Volocopter are targeting commercial launch within 12–24 months. Yet market readiness data remains fragmented, incomplete, and inaccessible.

  • Fragmented FAA data. LAANC authorizations, UAS facility maps, and Part 135 certifications are scattered across multiple federal databases with no unified view.
  • Varying state and local laws. UAM legislation differs dramatically by state — Texas passed comprehensive enabling legislation while other states have no framework at all. Tracking 50 legislatures is a full-time job.
  • Unverified operator announcements. Press releases and partnerships are announced frequently but rarely cross-referenced against actual FAA filings, SEC disclosures, or permit applications.
  • No unified source of truth. Investors, city planners, and operators each maintain their own spreadsheets. There is no independent readiness standard for air mobility.

AirIndex solves this.
