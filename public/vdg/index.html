How we work

Every market that matters in the next 20 years is visible in public data today. The signals exist before mainstream awareness. We build the infrastructure to detect them.

01

Ingest

Continuous monitoring of regulatory filings, legislative activity, operator disclosures, and infrastructure data from verified primary sources.

02

Classify

Every record classified by event type, relevance, and market attribution. Structured for auditability and reproducibility.

03

Score

Scored, tiered, and ranked intelligence updated automatically when qualifying signals are detected. Every change is sourced and traceable to its origin record.

Intelligence infrastructure

Every score we publish is backed by a five-container intelligence system — structured, auditable, and traceable from source document to market score.

FKB

Factor Knowledge Base

Factor definitions, weights, per-market scores with confidence levels. The scoring engine reads from FKB at runtime — weight changes propagate across the platform without code deployment.

RPL

Regulatory Precedent Library

Structured regulatory documents — federal rules, state legislation, FAA orders — with factor mappings, momentum tracking, and significance weighting. Auto-populated daily from the ingestion pipeline.

MCS

Market Context Store

State-level enforcement posture, regional clusters, and peer market groupings. Powers benchmarking and gap analysis across markets.

OID

Operator Intelligence Database

Operator deployment stages, certifications, financing history, and vertiport commitments. Six-stage deployment model from watchlist to commercial operations.

FPIS

Federal Programs Intelligence Store

Federal grant and SBIR programs mapped to scoring factors. Surfaces funding pathways in gap analysis — when a market has a readiness gap, FPIS identifies federal programs that address it, with peer city award precedents.

Published methodology, standardized terminology, and full source traceability available at airindex.io/methodology and airindex.io/terminology.
