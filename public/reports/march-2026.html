Executive Summary

March 2026 marks a pivotal month for the US eVTOL industry. The White House announced a federal eVTOL pilot program selecting Florida, New York, and Texas as the first three states for commercial powered-lift integration — with both Joby and Archer accepted into the FAA's Enterprise Integration Pilot Program (eIPP). Joby simultaneously announced its Uber Air Taxi booking integration, enabling ride-hail style eVTOL booking directly through the Uber app — the first consumer-facing air taxi marketplace at scale.

Meanwhile, the Archer vs. Joby legal battle escalated with Archer filing a countersuit alleging Joby's undisclosed China supply chain dependencies. While the litigation introduces uncertainty around individual operators, it paradoxically validates the competitive intensity of the market — both companies are racing to be first.

On the data integrity front, AirIndex completed a comprehensive scoring audit on March 7, correcting four markets (Las Vegas, Phoenix, Austin, Houston) where scoring factors were not supported by verifiable public records. This reflects our commitment to treating readiness scores as a ratings product — precision over promotion.

The platform now tracks 857 total ingested records across Federal Register, LegiScan (50 states + DC), SEC EDGAR, and operator news. Our new auto-discovery pipeline automatically identifies UAM signals for cities outside our tracked 21 markets, with Cincinnati, OH as the first auto-detected lead.

Top Stories — March 2026

White House eVTOL Pilot Program — FL, NY, TX Selected

The White House announced a federal eVTOL integration pilot program selecting Florida, New York, and Texas as the first three states for commercial powered-lift operations. Both Joby Aviation and Archer Aviation were accepted into the FAA's Enterprise Integration Pilot Program (eIPP), replacing the original UAS Integration Pilot Program. This is the strongest federal signal yet that eVTOL commercial service is moving from testing to deployment — and it directly validates three of AirIndex's top-5 scoring states. Joby's selection covers multi-state operations, while Archer's application includes corridors in all three states.

IMPACT: FLORIDA • NEW YORK • TEXAS • FEDERAL MILESTONE

Uber Air Taxi Booking Platform Revealed

Joby and Uber unveiled the consumer-facing air taxi booking platform, allowing riders to book eVTOL flights directly through the Uber app. This is the first time a major ride-hail platform has integrated air taxi service at a product level. While initially launching in markets where Joby has commercial authorization, the Uber integration represents the most significant consumer distribution channel for eVTOL services globally.

IMPACT: LOS ANGELES • NEW YORK • MIAMI

Archer vs. Joby Legal Escalation

Archer Aviation filed a countersuit against Joby Aviation, alleging that Joby defrauded the U.S. government by concealing its reliance on Chinese supply chain components. Joby's response is pending. While this introduces operator-level risk, it also signals the competitive intensity of the market — both companies are fighting over first-mover advantage in a market they believe is imminent.

IMPACT: OPERATOR RISK • REGULATORY UNCERTAINTY

AirIndex Data Audit — 4 Markets Corrected

AirIndex completed a comprehensive data audit on March 7, correcting Las Vegas (-50 pts), Phoenix (-30 pts), Austin (-15 pts), and Houston (-15 pts). Scoring factors that could not be verified against primary public records were removed. This is consistent with our ratings agency model — we err on the side of under-scoring rather than over-promoting markets.
